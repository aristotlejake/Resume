Career Profile

My interest in bleeding edge devices directed me to the relatively new field of study known as digital forensics. Dixie State University developed a digital forensics program which was one of the first of its kind; the DSU-Computer Crime Institute, throughout which I learned the fundamentals of hardware, file systems, data structures, operating systems, software, and networking principles. I also learned the legal processes and procedures of computer systems in relation to cyber crime.

After graduating, I applied that knowledge to a career in Information Technology where I quickly excelled to a leadership role and furthered my knowledge of computer systems in the modern enterprise.

As the world has become intrinsically reliant on technology, I have focused my career on the availability, reliability, integrity, and security of information in enterprise environments.
